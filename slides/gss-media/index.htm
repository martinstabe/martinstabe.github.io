Essential characteristics

  • Tell a story...
  • ...that cannot be told using simpler means
  • ...using data analysis that is integral to the reporting
  • ... and of ongoing value.

1. Interactive story-telling

An interactive news application must explain a topic with anarrative structure, not just display data for the reader to explore.

Explanatory and exploratory

Highlighting personal relevancy

2. Avoid interactivity wherever possible

Interactivity is not a goal in itself.

Interactivity shifts cognitive burden onto the reader

Use simpler, static versions wherever possible

Avoid ‘advent calendars’ or ‘spot-the-football’.

3. Information, not ornamentation

“Using data to add depth, colour or context to a story is one thing, but creating a story ex nihilo is where the real value lies ...

“Statistical journalism offers a ... way [to be] first, right and leaving rivals scrambling to report on your story.”

— John Burn-Murdoch in Data Journalism: Mapping the Future

4. Build something of lasting value

Introduce functionality or tell an ongoing story that can attract readers for longer than any one news item, ideally indefinitely.

Rule of thumb: It should retain value to readers for longer than it takes to produce.
